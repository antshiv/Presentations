The MLP's "Deliberation Room"

Why Go to a Higher Dimension and Back?

tangled_yarn

1. Input: Tangled Features

[B, T, C]

In the original dimension, concepts are mixed. It's hard to separate "river bank" from "financial bank" with a simple rule.

brain_gears

2. Expansion: Deliberation

[B, T, 4C]

By expanding to a higher dimension, the MLP creates space to "un-tangle" the features. Here, it can learn complex, non-linear relationships. "River bank" and "financial bank" are mapped to very different locations in this new, larger space.

organized_files

3. Contraction: Verdict

[B, T, C]

The model projects this newly clarified understanding back to the original dimension. The output is a refined representation where the meaning is now clearer.

The Technical Implication: Linear Separability

This process is analogous to the "kernel trick" in SVMs. Patterns that are not separable by a line in 2D may become separable by a plane in 3D. The MLP's expansion serves a similar purpose: it projects features into a space where complex patterns become easier to isolate and process. The higher dimension is where the "hard thinking" happens.
