What is Profile power?

Profile power is the power required to overcome the drag of the rotor blades as they move through the air.

Blade Element Theory

  • Blade element theory is a mathematical process for calculating the forces on a rotor blade in forward flight.
  • It is based on the assumption that the blade is made up of a number of small sections, each of which can be considered to be in steady flow.
  • Each section is then analysed to determine the forces acting on it.
  • These forces are then integrated along the length of the blade to give the total forces acting on the blade.

Rotor solidity is a dimensionless quantity used in design and analysis of rotorcraft, propellers and wind turbines. Rotor solidity is a function of the aspect ratio and number of blades in the rotor and is widely used as a parameter for ensuring geometric similarity in rotorcraft experiments. It provides a measure of how close a lifting rotor system is to an ideal actuator disk in momentum theory. It also plays an important role in determining the fluid speed across the rotor disk when lift is generated and consequentially the performance of the rotor; amount of downwash around it, and noise levels the rotor generates. It is also used to compare performance characteristics between rotors of different sizes. Typical values of rotor solidity ratio for helicopters fall in the range 0.05 to 0.12.[1] Source: Rotor Solidity Wikipedia

FOM Revisited

Use FOM as a rule of thumb to compare different rotorcrafts. But be cautious of the assumptions.
    Do not compare if:
  • Rotors that have different disk loadings and different airfoils will have different FOMs.
  • Rotors operating at different altitudes will have different FOMs.
