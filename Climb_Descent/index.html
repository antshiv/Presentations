What is Momentum Theory?

  • It is a theory that describes the flow field around a rotor in hover.
  • It is based on the conservation of momentum.
  • It is a simple theory that can be used to estimate the thrust and power required to hover.

Reality Simplification

  • Flow is unsteady
  • Flow is 3D
  • Flow is compressible
  • Flow is viscous
  • Tip vortices
  • Blockage effects

Some assumptions to simplifiy calculations

  • Flow is steady
  • Flow is 1D
  • Flow is incompressible - (Density Constant)
  • Flow is inviscid

Equation Manipulation

\[ \begin{aligned} TV_i = \frac{1}{2}(\rho A_{\infty} w ) w^2 \\ T = \rho A_{\infty} w^{2} \\ (\rho A_{\infty} w^{2})V_i = \frac{1}{2}(\rho A_{\infty} w ) w^2 \\ w = 2 V_i \\ \\ AV_i = A_{\infty}( 2 V_i) \\ A_{\infty} = \frac{A}{2} \\ \\ T = \rho (\frac{A}{2}) (2 V_i)^{2} \\ T = 2 \rho A V_i^{2} \\ v_i = \sqrt{\frac{T}{2} \rho A} \\ V_i = \sqrt{\frac{D.L}{2 \rho A}} \\ \\ \\ P = TV_i \\ P = 2 \rho A V_i^{2} V_i \\ P = 2 \rho A V_i^{3} \\ P = \frac{T ^(\frac{3}{2})} { \sqrt{2 \rho A} }\\ \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} w = 2 V_i \\ \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} A_{\infty} = \frac{A}{2} \\ \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} v_i = \sqrt{T/2 \rho A} \\ \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} V_i = \sqrt{D.L/2 \rho A} \\ \end{aligned} \]
\[ \begin{aligned} P = T^(3/2) / \sqrt{2 \rho A} \\ \end{aligned} \]

Analysis

Equation Insight
\[ \begin{aligned} w = 2 V_i \\ \end{aligned} \] Induced velocity is twice the velocity of the rotor
\[ \begin{aligned} A_{\infty} = \frac{A}{2} \\ \end{aligned} \] Area of the rotor is half the area of the actuator disk
\[ \begin{aligned} V_i = \sqrt{\frac{D.L}{2 \rho A}} \\ \end{aligned} \] Induced velocity is proportional to the square root of disk loading
\[ \begin{aligned} PL = (V_i)^-1 = \sqrt{\frac{2 \rho A}{D.L}} \\ \end{aligned} \] Power loading is inversely proportional to the square root of disk loading
\[ \begin{aligned} P = \frac{T^(\frac{3}{2})}{\sqrt{2 \rho A}} \\ \end{aligned} \] Power required is proportional to the cube of thrust and inversely proportional to the density

Figure of Merit (Hover efficiency)

\[ \begin{aligned} FOM = \frac{\text{Ideal power required to hover}}{\text{Actual power required to hover}} \\ \end{aligned} \]
  • Unlike power loading, FOM is non-dimensional
  • Has a value of 0 and 1
\[ \begin{aligned} FOM = \frac{\frac{T^(\frac{3}{2})}{\sqrt{2 \rho A}}} {K (\frac{T^(\frac{3}{2})}{\sqrt{2 \rho A}}) + P_o} \\ \end{aligned} \]
Symbol Meaning

A Area of the rotor
K Induced power factor (Loss Factor)
