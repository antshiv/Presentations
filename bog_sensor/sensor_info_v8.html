DIY Soil Probe: The Hardware Store Prototype Guide

Building a Low-Cost, Field-Testable Sensor

1. Your "Rev 1" Shopping List (Total: ~$50)

Electrical Aisle

  • RG-58 or RG-6 Coax Cable
  • Braided Ground Strap

Plumbing Aisle

  • 1/2" Copper Pipe (~2 ft)
  • 1/2" Copper End Cap

Fastener Aisle

  • 1/4"-20 Stainless Threaded Rod
  • 1/4"-20 Stainless Nuts/Washers
  • 1/4"-20 Stainless Coupling Nuts

2. Component Breakdown & Assembly

Exploded Assembly View

A. The Center Rod (Stainless Steel)

This is your signal conductor. Must be stainless steel to prevent rust. Good options are threaded rods, the shaft of a long carriage bolt, or a welding rod.

B. The Outer Shield

This is your ground return. A 1/2" copper pipe is rigid and easy to work with. Alternatively, you can strip the braided shield from a cheap RG-6 coax cable.

C. Assembly Hack: Centering the Rod

The rod must be perfectly centered. You can 3D print small centering rings, or simply wrap the rod every few inches with electrical tape until it fits snugly inside the outer shield.

D. The Replaceable Tip

For a durable, replaceable tip, JB Weld a stainless coupling nut to the bottom of your main rod. This allows you to thread in different tips, like a hardened bolt ground to a point.

E. CRITICAL CONNECTION: Shield to Ground Plane

This is the most important electrical joint. The outer shield (copper pipe or braid) MUST have a solid, low-resistance connection to the ground plane (copper end cap).

  1. Flare the end of the braid slightly.
  2. Place the copper end cap firmly over the flared braid.
  3. Apply flux and solder the cap directly to the braid/pipe for a permanent electrical and mechanical bond.
  4. Clean the joint and seal with heat shrink tubing.

3. Quick Bench Test Procedure

Before going to the field, verify your TDR electronics are working with these simple tests. You should see a clear and distinct change in the reflection time or amplitude for each step.

1. Dry Sand

Gives a baseline reading. Expect a small, fast reflection.

2. Wet Sand

Confirms water sensitivity. Expect a larger, significantly delayed reflection.

3. Potting Soil

Expect multiple small reflections as the wave hits inhomogeneous roots/clumps.

4. Water

Highest permittivity. Expect a very large reflection right at the probe's surface.

This guide provides a low-cost pathway to building a functional TDR soil probe prototype for testing and validation.
